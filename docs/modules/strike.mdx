---
title: Strike
description: "A dual, clean, zero-bleed, envelope-driven low-pass gate."
---

**Strike** is a clean low-pass gate: a struck, percussive voice with **no tone bleeding
through a closed gate**. It's a dual module — two identical channels (**A** / **B**),
each polyphonic up to 16 voices — built around a shaped, dual-mode envelope rather than a
vactrol.

## Signal flow

A **HIT** fires the envelope, which opens the gate and decays. Audio at **IN** passes
through the gate to **OUT**. Because **IN is normalled to a DC level**, a HIT with nothing
patched into IN emits the raw envelope as a **percussive ping** — Strike is a drum voice on
its own.

## Controls

Per channel:

- **OPEN** _(fader)_ — the gate floor: how open the gate sits at rest, before any strike.
  Down = shut, up = open.
- **DECAY** _(fader)_ — how long the strike rings out. Watch the **openness meter** between
  the two faders: it fills on a HIT and **falls at the decay rate**, so short decays snap
  down and long decays ooze down the ladder.
- **MATERIAL** _(knob)_ — a continuous **hard → soft** morph of the struck body's character
  (brightness and shape). The hero control.
- **DEC** / **CTRL** _(attenuverters)_ — bipolar (±) amounts for the two CV inputs directly
  below them. Centre = off.

## Inputs & outputs

| Jack | Role |
| --- | --- |
| **IN** | Audio in. Normalled to DC, so a HIT alone pings. |
| **HIT** | Trigger — fires the envelope (Schmitt-triggered, so noisy gates don't chatter). |
| **DEC** | Decay CV (scaled by the DEC attenuverter above it). |
| **CTRL** | Pre-envelope control / level. Normalled to **+10 V** (attenuverter at 0 ⇒ no effect). |
| **OUT** | The gated signal. |

The **meter** between OPEN and DECAY shows the first voice's gate openness — your live
window on the envelope.

## Analog drift

Strike's analog character — tiny per-instance tolerance and drift — is **always on**. It's
part of the voice, not an option, so "clean" never tips into "sterile."

<Note>
  Need bit-identical, reproducible output (phase-coherent stacking, precise sound design)?
  Right-click the module → **Deterministic (disable analog drift)**. It's persisted per
  instance.
</Note>

## Polyphony

Each channel follows the channel count of its **IN** and **HIT** — patch a poly cable and
every voice gets its own gate. OUT is polyphonic to match.
