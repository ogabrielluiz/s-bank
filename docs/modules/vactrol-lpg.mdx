title: Vactrol LPG

**Vactrol LPG** is a virtual-analog **Buchla-292-style** low-pass gate. It models the
opto-isolator (vactrol) at the heart of the 292: feed it a control voltage and you get the
characteristic **slow attack and long, bloomed release** — the lag and ring are inherent to
the circuit model, not an added envelope. Polyphonic.

## What's modelled

The audio path is the Parker & D'Angelo **state-space cell** of the real 292, and the
control path is the authors' exact CV → LED-current circuit (bias stage, Lambert-W
approximation, saturating branches). The resonance, drive, and the vactrol's asymmetric
attack/release all come out of that model.

## Controls

- **RESO** _(knob)_ — resonance / emphasis at the gate's corner. Pushes the 292 toward its
  resonant, vocal edge.
- **DRIVE** _(knob)_ — input drive into the nonlinear cell — from clean to saturated.
- **MODE** _(switch)_ — the response:
  - **Both** — combined low-pass + VCA (the classic LPG "pluck").
  - **VCA** — amplitude only.
  - **Lowpass** — filter only.
- **OS** _(switch)_ — oversampling **1× / 2× / 4×** for the delay-free solve. Higher =
  cleaner at the cost of CPU.

## Inputs & outputs

| Jack | Role |
| --- | --- |
| **IN** | Audio in. |
| **CV** | Control voltage — drives the vactrol (gate / envelope / pluck source). |
| **OUT** | The gated, filtered signal. |

<Tip>
  For the classic Buchla pluck, set **MODE → Both** and feed **CV** a short trigger or
  envelope — the vactrol's bloom does the rest.
</Tip>

## Polyphony

OUT follows the channel count of the audio **IN**; each voice runs its own vactrol cell.
