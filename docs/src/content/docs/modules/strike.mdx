**Strike** is a **demo module**, not the product. It exists to drive the library's
clean-gate blocks inside VCV Rack and show how they behave under a panel — the reusable
C++ underneath is what S-Bank ships. Everything the panel does, it does by feeding
`sbank::StrikeCore`; nothing in the sound lives in the Rack layer.

The clean gate is the inverse of the [vactrol cell](modules/vactrol-lpg): zero bleed when
closed, driven by a shaped envelope rather than an opto-isolator, with a whisper of seeded
drift so "clean" never tips into "sterile." Each strike fires a fast/slow envelope pair
into a TPT state-variable low-pass; **Material** morphs the attack and cutoff ceiling from
hard to soft, and a built-in pitch tracker stretches longer notes' decays so they ring in
proportion.

<Aside type="note">
  The voice is `sbank::StrikeCore` — header-only C++ with **no VCV-Rack-SDK dependency**.
  The same construct/`setParams`/`processSample` sequence the panel runs is what you call
  from any audio project. See the [library reference](library) for the building blocks.
</Aside>

## At a glance

Strike is **dual** — two independent channels, **A** and **B**, mirrored on a 16 HP panel —
and each channel is **polyphonic** (up to 16 voices). A channel's voice count follows its
audio **IN** and **HIT** inputs, and **OUT** matches that channel count.

## Controls

Per channel, top to bottom:

- **OPEN** — a fader setting the gate floor: how far open the gate sits at rest, before any
  hit. At 0% the channel is silent until struck; raise it to bleed a continuous level
  through.
- **DECAY** — a fader setting how long the gate rings out after a hit. The openness **meter**
  rises in the gap **between** the OPEN and DECAY faders: it fills on a hit and falls at the
  decay rate, so you watch the strike ring out. Bottom segment lights first.
- **MATERIAL** — a knob morphing the struck character from **HARD** to **SOFT**. It moves the
  attack time and the cutoff ceiling together: hard is fast and bright, soft is slower and
  darker.
- **DEC** — a bipolar CV attenuverter sitting directly above the DEC input jack. It scales
  decay modulation; at the center detent the CV has no effect. Turn counter-clockwise to
  invert.
- **CTRL** — a bipolar CV attenuverter above the CTRL input jack, scaling the control input
  that opens the gate directly. CTRL is normalled to +10 V, so with the attenuverter at 0
  there is no effect; turn it up to push the gate open from the normal, or patch a CV to
  drive it.

## Inputs &amp; outputs

Per channel (A and B identical):

| Jack | Dir | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **IN** | in | Audio in. Normalled to a DC level, so a **HIT** with nothing patched here pings the envelope out as a percussive tone. |
| **HIT** | in | Trigger. Schmitt-triggered (hysteretic), so a noisy or slow gate can't chatter-retrigger. A rising edge fires the strike. |
| **DEC** | in | Decay CV, scaled by the **DEC** attenuverter. Additive — no effect when unpatched. |
| **CTRL** | in | Control CV that opens the gate, scaled by the **CTRL** attenuverter. Normalled to +10 V. |
| **OUT** | out | The gated audio. Channel count follows IN / HIT. |

<Aside type="tip">
  Because **IN** is normalled to DC, an unpatched channel is already a percussion voice:
  patch a clock or trigger to **HIT**, dial **MATERIAL** and **DECAY**, and each pulse pings
  the envelope. Patch audio into **IN** and the same hit gates that signal instead.
</Aside>

## Analog drift

The seeded analog-dirt layer — small per-voice tolerances plus a slow drift on the decay,
and a pink-noise floor — is **always on**. It is part of the instrument's voice, not a
toggle to switch on. Each voice seeds its own deterministic imperfection, so the character
is stable across reloads but never identical voice-to-voice.

When you need the sound to be reproducible — golden regressions, sample-accurate stacking,
or bit-identical renders — right-click the module and choose **Deterministic (disable
analog drift)**. That setting is saved with the patch.

## Driving the voice from C++

The panel is a thin wrapper; here is the same voice headless, no Rack SDK in sight:

```cpp
#include "dsp/SBankDSP.hpp"

sbank::StrikeCore strike(48000.f);          // sample rate
strike.setParams(/*open=*/0.f, /*decay=*/0.5f, /*material=*/0.f);

float peak = 0.f;
for (int i = 0; i < 96000; ++i) {
    const float hit = i < 10 ? 5.f : 0.f;   // a single trigger edge
    // processSample(audioIn, ctrl01, decayMod, hitV)
    const float y = strike.processSample(1.f, 0.f, 0.f, hit);
    peak = std::max(peak, y);
}
```

<Steps>

1. Construct a `StrikeCore` with your sample rate (and optionally a seed for the drift).

2. Call `setParams(open, decay, material)` whenever a control changes — all three are
   normalized 0..1, matching the panel's faders and knob.

3. Per sample, call `processSample(audioIn, ctrl01, decayMod, hitV)`: the audio in (the
   panel feeds a DC normal when IN is unpatched), the 0..1 control level, an additive decay
   modulation, and the hit voltage. The panel runs the HIT through a Schmitt trigger first —
   pass a clean edge.

4. Feed `lastControl()` to a meter, and call `setImperfection(...)` / `reset()` exactly as
   the [library reference](library) describes to match a Rack channel.

</Steps>
